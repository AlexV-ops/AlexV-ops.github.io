Features of the Czech Republic

The Czech Republic is one of the most culturally rich countries to visit in Central Europe. Visit untouched forests and unforgettable landscapes, or enjoy dynamic cities with a mix of old and new.

History

The modern country of Czechia has historically been known as Bohemia, a landlocked country between Germany and Austria. It has changed hands many times over the centuries, from the Holy Roman Empire to the Habsburg monarchy to the Austro-Hungarian Empire. (Here's an example of a red dotted overline.)

After the collapse of the Austro-Hungarian Empire, Czechia briefly enjoyed independence as the First Czechoslovak Republic, before being conquered by Germany in 1938, and by the Eastern Bloc (USSR) after World War II. Communist rule would continue until 1989, and the nation eventually separated into the modern nations of the Czech Republic and Slovakia. (Here's an example of a green wavy underline.)
